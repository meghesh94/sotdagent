# SOTD Taste DNA

This file defines the soul of Song of the Day. It drives search queries, filtering, and curation decisions. Edit this to steer what the agent finds.

---

## Who We Are

SOTD (Song of the Day) shares one carefully curated song daily with subscribers. Every song must be worth pausing your day for. We're not an algorithm — we're a friend with great taste texting you a song.

---

## Taste Pillars

### 1. Emotional Resonance
Every song must make you FEEL something specific. Not background music. Not "nice." It should hit.
- **Examples:** Bloom (vulnerability), The Night We Met (nostalgia/heartbreak), Cold Little Heart (slow-burn soul), Apocalypse (tender melancholy)

### 2. Cross-Cultural Fluency
We move between English indie, Bollywood, Sufi, Pakistani indie, classic Hindi, French, EDM — the thread isn't language, it's emotional authenticity.
- **Examples:** Husna (Sufi folk), Arabesque (Coldplay), Baby It's Cold Outside (jazz), Ghalat Fehmi (Urdu indie)

### 3. Discovery-First
Not mainstream Top 40. We want songs people haven't heard but SHOULD hear. The sweet spot: quality of a hit, obscurity of a hidden gem.
- **Examples:** Houndmouth, Rainbow Kitten Surprise, Still Corners, Fazerdaze, Kaptan, Naalayak

### 4. "Stomp and Holler" Energy
A signature SOTD vibe: anthemic indie folk that makes you want to drive with windows down.
- **Examples:** Mumford & Sons, Lord Huron, The Lumineers, WILD, Of Monsters and Men, Houndmouth

### 5. Timeless Classics (occasional)
Respect the old masters. Not every day, but when we do, it lands.
- **Examples:** Beatles, Ella Fitzgerald, Lata Mangeshkar, Eric Clapton, Hemant Kumar

### 6. Indie Hindi/Urdu — New Wave
The Indian independent music scene is core to SOTD's identity. There are TWO layers:
- **Established indie:** Anuv Jain, Prateek Kuhad, The Yellow Diary, Naalayak, The Local Train
- **Raw new wave (2023+):** Banjaare, Sparsh, Prabhakar Raj, Coke Studio Bharat artists. Less polished, more intimate, emotionally direct. This is where the scene is heading — prioritize finding more of these.

### 7. Cinematic / Atmospheric
Songs that feel like movie soundtracks. Dreamy, layered, transportive.
- **Examples:** M83, Beach House, Sufjan Stevens, Cigarettes After Sex, Explosions in the Sky

### 8. Small Artists with Big Hearts
Tiny artists (<500K views) who make SOTD-quality music. These are the best discoveries — songs your audience literally cannot find on their own.
- **Examples:** Beluga Lagoon (Hammer & Bones), Daniel Leggs (Runaway), Kingfishr, Amble, Sara Beth

### 9. Emotional Pop-Storytelling
Not all pop is banned. If it's genuinely personal, story-driven, and emotionally raw — even with millions of views — it can be SOTD.
- **Examples:** Alex Warren (Ordinary), Seafret (Atlantis)

---

## What Makes a Song SOTD-Worthy

- [ ] Would you text this to a friend unprompted?
- [ ] Does it have a "moment" — a build, a lyric, a feeling that sticks?
- [ ] Is it discoverable enough that most people haven't heard it?
- [ ] Can you write 2-3 sentences about WHY this song matters?
- [ ] Does it fit at least one Taste Pillar above?

---

## What to AVOID

- Generic Top 40 hits everyone already knows (Shape of You, Counting Stars)
- Songs without emotional weight — technically fine but forgettable
- Deep cuts that are too obscure / low production quality
- DJ mixes, compilations, sleep music, meditation tracks
- Covers unless they genuinely reimagine the original
- Songs already in the 172-song library (auto-deduplicated)

## Feedback Log

Small, cumulative learnings from each session. These compound over time.

### Session 1 (2026-04-11)
**Test:** Picked 9 songs from Rajat's 200-track YT mix. Asked him to rate.
**Results:**
- Cheap Red Wine — Eddie Dalton: **7/10** (Irish folk, simple, honest, clear melody)
- Wreckless Heart — Glen Hansard: **5/10** (too intense/raw for SOTD?)
- Saajan Mhara, Waqt Ne Kiya, Driftwood, Nothing Could Tear Us Apart, O re piya, Baatein, Nuages: all **2/10**

**Learnings (small nudges, not rewrites):**
- Simple + honest + clear melody > atmospheric/vague. A song needs a HOOK, not just a mood.
- "Cross-cultural discovery" isn't automatic — Rajasthani folk, French world music, classic Bollywood reimaginations didn't land. The cross-cultural picks that work (Husna, Bairan, Ghalat Fehmi) succeed because of raw emotion, not novelty.
- Irish/Celtic indie folk direction has potential (Cheap Red Wine 7/10) but don't over-cluster into one lane.
- Be cautious with "reimagined classics" — the originals are sacred, covers/reworks need to be exceptional.
- Don't over-index on "atmospheric" as a quality. SOTD songs need to grab you in the first 30 seconds, not build for 2 minutes.

### Session 2 (2026-04-11)
**Test:** Picked 5 songs from discovery run, leaning heavily into Irish/Celtic indie folk.
**Result:** "None of these, too similar — nothing special about these."
- Killeagh (Kingfishr), Caroline (Kingfishr), Featherstone (The Paper Kites), Candy (Paolo Nutini), Dream Catch Me (Newton Faulkner): all rejected.

**Learnings:**
- ONE good rating in a lane (Cheap Red Wine 7/10) does NOT mean "give me 5 more from the same lane." Diversity across picks is critical.
- "Nothing special" = genre-matching is not enough. Each song in the SOTD library is the BEST version of its thing. Bloom isn't just indie folk — it's a masterpiece about vulnerability. Cold Little Heart isn't just soul — it's a 10-minute slow burn. The bar is EXCEPTIONAL, not "fits the genre."
- The 5 picks must each come from a DIFFERENT mood/pillar. Never cluster.
- Finding songs I can't actually listen to is the core limitation. The curation layer needs to prioritize songs with strong critical/emotional reputation, not just genre proximity.

### Session 3 (2026-04-12)
**Test:** First Gemini-powered discovery run. Gemini listened to 11 songs (hit free tier rate limit at 20 req/day), curated top 5.
**Picks presented:**
1. Chaap Tilak — Abida Parveen & Rahat Fateh Ali Khan (9.5/10)
2. Tu Aake Dekhle — King (9/10)
3. Iktara (MTV Unplugged) — Amit Trivedi, Kavita Seth (9/10)
4. Roar of Narsimha — Sam C.S. (8/10)
5. Better — Kodaline (8/10)

**Result:** "The first 3 are very very common. Too common. We can never send these."

**Learnings:**
- Gemini can judge QUALITY brilliantly but has NO sense of how mainstream a song is. Chaap Tilak, Tu Aake Dekhle, and Iktara are all exceptional songs — but everyone's already heard them. SOTD is about discovery, not curation of known greats.
- **Popularity must be a hard filter, not a soft preference.** A 10/10 song that's already famous is a 0/10 for SOTD. The scoring formula must penalize fame heavily.
- The Gemini prompt now asks for a popularity estimate (1-5 scale) and caps SOTD scores for well-known songs.
- Roar of Narsimha (cinematic/devotional) and Better (Kodaline) were not rated — pending feedback.
